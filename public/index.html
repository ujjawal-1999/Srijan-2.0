About the Event

“SRiJaN 2.0” is the National Innovation and Entrepreneurship Summit organised by E Cell NIT Silchar. It is a unique initiative to motivate and educate people about entrepreneurship and to provide a meeting ground for Corporate experts and young budding entrepreneurs from NIT Silchar as well as several distinguished institutions across the country.

Organized by the Institute Innovation Council & Start-up Centre, NIT SILCHAR in association with IEEE ComSoc NITS Student Branch Chapter

About NIT SILCHAR

National Institute Of Technology, Silchar (राष्ट्रीय प्रौद्योगिकी संस्थान, सिलचर) is one of the 30 National Institutes of Technology of India and was established in 1967 as a Regional Engineering College in Assam. In 2002 it was upgraded to the status of National Institute of Technology and was declared as Institute of National Importance under the National Institutes of Technology Act, 2007.

Outwit the Board Room

We understand it’s difficult to come up with an idea, but an idea is not all you need. Put your analytic skills to the test and improve on a pre-existing idea. Objective/Outcomes

The event forces the team to research and dive deep into the business model of a given company, tests their analytical observations and promotes critical thinking and teamwork. Most of all, it promotes a spirit of improvement and learning based on others' experiences. It promotes a better understanding of finances, and promotes decision making under the pressure of knowing those decisions that affect people beyond their comprehension.

Cyber Security Workshop

Cyber security is the act of safeguarding protecting data, networks, programs and other information from malicious attacks.

The cybersecurity industry is rapidly growing every day, with new technological developments comes new threats. Although more resources are being deployed to counter cyber-attacks, the nature of the industry still has a long way to go before we can, as a whole, catch up with these threats.

Here are some Alarming Cyber Security Facts and Stats:

  • There is a hacker attack every 39 seconds.
  • On average, only 5% of companies’ folders are properly protected.
  • Since COVID-19, the US FBI reported a 300% increase in reported cybercrimes
  • Approximately $6 trillion is expected to be spent globally on cybersecurity by 2021


Do you feel vulnerable that your data may go in wrong hands?

Here at SRIJAN 2.0 the cyber security workshop we will assist you on how to save yourself from various types of cyber threats & will also act as a stepping stone to an exciting career in Cyber Security.
